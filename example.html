What is the Pink Tax?

Pink Tax refers to the extra amount women are charged for certain products or services — even when they're nearly identical to men's versions. It's a hidden cost that affects financial independence and equity.

Make Your Voice Heard

A 2015 study by the New York City Department of Consumer Affairs found that products marketed to women cost an average of 7% more than similar products marketed to men. This pricing disparity was observed in categories like toys, clothing, personal care items, and adult products.

Over a lifetime, women may pay thousands of rupees more for essential goods and services simply due to gendered pricing. From razors and deodorants to haircuts and dry cleaning, this accumulated cost contributes to the economic inequality faced by women.


Despite the name, the Pink Tax is not an official tax, but a systemic market pricing issue. It reflects how companies brand and price products differently for women, even when the items are functionally identical to those for men..
